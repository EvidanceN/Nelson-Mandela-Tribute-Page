Nelson Mandela

The first black President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999

Nelson Mandela
  • 1918 (July 18): Nelson Mandela is born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa's Cape Province.
  • 1943: Mandela joins the African National Congress (ANC), a political organization working against apartheid.
  • 1944: Mandela, along with other ANC leaders, helps form the African National Congress Youth League (ANCYL).
  • 1952: Mandela becomes the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the Defiance Campaign against unjust apartheid laws.
  • 1956: Mandela and 155 others are arrested and charged with high treason. The trial lasts until 1961, but all defendants are acquitted.
  • 1961: The ANC adopts a more militant approach, forming an armed wing known as Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK), with Mandela as its commander.
  • 1962: Mandela is arrested and convicted of incitement and leaving the country without a passport. He is sentenced to five years in prison.
  • 1963 (June 12): Mandela and several other MK leaders are arrested during a police raid on a farm in Rivonia. This leads to the famous Rivonia Trial.
  • 1964 (June 11): Mandela and seven others are sentenced to life in prison during the Rivonia Trial. He begins his imprisonment on Robben Island.
  • 1982 (December 9): Mandela is moved to Pollsmoor Prison on the mainland.
  • 1990 (February 11): President F.W. de Klerk announces the unbanning of the ANC and other anti-apartheid organizations. Mandela is released from prison on February 11 after 27 years.
  • 1993: Mandela and de Klerk are jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to dismantle the apartheid system peacefully.
  • 1994 (April 27-28): South Africa holds its first democratic elections. Nelson Mandela becomes the first black president of South Africa.
  • 1999: Mandela steps down as president after serving one term.
  • 2004: Mandela announces his retirement from public life.
  • 2013 (December 5): Nelson Mandela passes away at the age of 95 in Johannesburg.
